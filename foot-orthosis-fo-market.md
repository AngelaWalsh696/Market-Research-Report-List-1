<p><p>The Foot Orthosis (FO) market is divided into dynamic and static orthotics. Dynamic orthotics are designed to provide support while allowing some degree of movement, enhancing mobility and function for patients with conditions like flat feet or sports injuries. In contrast, static orthotics are intended for stabilization and pain relief, often used for individuals with more severe foot deformities or conditions requiring fixed support. Both types aim to improve patient comfort and biomechanical alignment, catering to diverse therapeutic needs.</p></p>
