<p><p>The cancer vaccines drug pipeline encompasses two primary market types: therapeutic and prophylactic vaccines. Therapeutic vaccines are designed to treat existing cancer by stimulating the immune system to attack cancer cells, often used in advanced stages or specific types of cancer. In contrast, prophylactic vaccines aim to prevent cancer from developing by targeting viral infections that can lead to cancer, such as HPV or Hepatitis B. Both types are crucial in advancing cancer prevention and treatment strategies in oncology.</p></p>
