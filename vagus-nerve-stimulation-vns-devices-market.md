<p><p>Vagus Nerve Stimulation (VNS) devices are categorized into implantable and external types. Implantable VNS devices are surgically placed under the skin, delivering electrical impulses to the vagus nerve to manage conditions like epilepsy and depression. External VNS devices, on the other hand, are non-invasive and typically used at home, utilizing electrodes placed on the skin to stimulate the vagus nerve without the need for surgery. Both types aim to enhance therapeutic outcomes by modulating nerve activity.</p></p>
