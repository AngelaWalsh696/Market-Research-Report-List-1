<p><p>The dental gel materials market is segmented into two main types: opaque and translucent gels. Opaque gels are designed to prevent light penetration, making them suitable for applications requiring privacy or masking effects, often used in procedures like whitening. Translucent gels, on the other hand, allow light to pass through, making them ideal for applications where visibility of underlying layers or tissues is essential, such as in certain treatment or adhesive processes. Each type serves distinct clinical needs and preferences.</p></p>
