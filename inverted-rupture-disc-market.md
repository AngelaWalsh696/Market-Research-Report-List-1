<p><p>An Inverted Rupture Disc is a critical safety device used in pressure relief systems to prevent over-pressurization and protect equipment from potential damage. It operates by rupturing at a predetermined pressure, allowing for the controlled release of gas or liquid. Unlike traditional rupture discs, inverted rupture discs are designed to function with the concave side facing the process pressure, which makes them ideal for certain applications where clean and precise venting is essential.</p><p>The Inverted Rupture Disc Market is witnessing substantial growth driven by increasing safety regulations across various industries, including oil and gas, chemical, and pharmaceuticals.
