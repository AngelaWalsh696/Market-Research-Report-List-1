Injection molding involves forcing molten PPS into a mold to create detailed shapes, suitable for complex designs and high-volume production. In contrast, extrusion involves pushing PPS through a die to create continuous profiles or strands, ideal for uniform cross-sections and longer products. Both methods leverage PPS's high thermal stability and chemical resistance, making them essential for applications in industries like automotive, electronics, and textiles.</p></p>
