<p><p>3D printing in the industrial market encompasses various technologies, each serving distinct applications. Stereolithography (SLA) offers high-resolution prototypes using liquid resin. Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) is prevalent for cost-effective plastic parts. Selective Laser Sintering (SLS) processes powdered materials into durable components. Direct Metal Laser Sintering (DMLS) enables complex metal designs. PolyJet Printing (MJP) and Inkjet Printing provide multi-material capabilities. Electron Beam Melting (EBM) focuses on aerospace applications, while Laser Metal Deposition (LMD) repairs components. Direct Light Projection (DLP) enhances speed and complexity in resin printing.</p></p>
