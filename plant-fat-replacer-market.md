<p><p>The plant fat replacer market comprises three main types: carbohydrate-based, protein-based, and lipid-based. Carbohydrate-based replacers utilize ingredients like fibers and starches to mimic fat's texture and mouthfeel, often found in baked goods. Protein-based replacers leverage plant proteins to enhance texture and provide a creamy consistency in dairy alternatives. Lipid-based replacers incorporate plant oils and fats to recreate the richness and flavor of traditional fats, suitable for spreads and sauces, ensuring a diverse range of applications in food products.</p></p>
