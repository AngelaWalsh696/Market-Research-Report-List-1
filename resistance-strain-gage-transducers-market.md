<p><p>Resistance strain gauge transducers measure deformation by detecting changes in electrical resistance. The foil strain gauge is widely used due to its durability and sensitivity, employing a thin metallic foil pattern. Wire strain gauges use fine wires to enhance sensitivity but are less common. Semiconductor strain gauges offer greater sensitivity than metal types, utilizing semiconductor materials to achieve higher gauge factors. Each type serves various applications in structural monitoring, aerospace, automotive, and industrial processes, catering to specific sensitivity and environmental requirements.</p></p>
