<p><p>Thermophotovoltaic (TPV) cells convert infrared radiation into electricity, and the market is segmented into epitaxial and non-epitaxial method cells. Epitaxial method cells involve growing a thin layer of semiconductor material on a substrate, enhancing efficiency and performance for high-temperature applications. In contrast, non-epitaxial method cells utilize bulk materials or simpler fabrication processes, often offering cost advantages but potentially lower efficiency. Both types cater to various applications, including waste heat recovery and concentrated solar power systems.</p></p>
