<p><p>The primary antibodies market is divided into two main types: polyclonal and monoclonal antibodies. Polyclonal antibodies are derived from multiple B cell sources, offering a diverse range of specificities for various epitopes, making them useful for applications requiring broad reactivity. Monoclonal antibodies, on the other hand, are produced by identical immune cells that are clones of a unique parent cell, providing high specificity and consistency, ideal for targeted applications and diagnostic purposes.</p></p>
