<p><p>Modified Atmosphere Packaging (MAP) is a technology used to extend the shelf life of perishable products by altering the composition of the gases surrounding the food. This involves replacing the air in the packaging with a specific mix of gases—usually carbon dioxide, oxygen, and nitrogen—to slow down the growth of microorganisms and oxidation processes. As consumer demand for fresh, minimally processed food rises, MAP has gained traction across various sectors, including fruits, vegetables, meats, and dairy products.</p><p>The Modified Atmosphere Packaging Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 12.7% during the forecast period, driven by increasing global population and a growing trend towards convenience foods. Sustainability is becoming a focal point as companies explore eco-friendly materials and practices. Additionally, the rise of e-commerce in food distribution is boosting demand for MAP solutions, ensuring that products remain fresh during transit. Overall, the MAP market is poised to advance significantly, reflecting broader trends towards health-conscious and convenient food options among consumers.</p></p>
