<p><p>Optical tweezers and magnetic tweezers are advanced tools used in mechanobiology to manipulate microscopic structures, such as cells and biomolecules, with high precision. Optical tweezers utilize focused laser beams to trap and move particles, while magnetic tweezers employ magnetic fields to exert forces on magnetic beads attached to biological samples. These technologies enable researchers to study mechanical properties, interactions, and dynamics at the nanoscale, providing insights into cellular processes and contributing to the advancement of biomedical research.</p></p>
