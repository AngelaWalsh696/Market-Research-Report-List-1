<p><p>The leukemia therapeutic market encompasses three primary treatment phases: induction therapy, consolidation therapy, and maintenance therapy. Induction therapy aims to achieve remission by rapidly reducing leukemic cells. Following this, consolidation therapy strengthens remission and eliminates residual disease to prevent relapse. Maintenance therapy then sustains remission over time, often utilizing lower doses to minimize side effects and prolong survival. Together, these therapies form a comprehensive approach to managing leukemia, addressing immediate needs and long-term disease control.</p></p>
