<p><p>Cathode active materials (CAM) for lithium-ion batteries are crucial for energy storage performance. Lithium Cobalt Oxide (LCO) offers high energy density but limited thermal stability. Lithium Manganese Oxide (LMO) provides better stability and lower cost. Lithium Iron Phosphate (LFP) is known for safety and longevity but has lower energy density. Lithium Nickel Cobalt Manganese Oxide (NMC) balances performance and cost, making it popular for electric vehicles. Lithium Nickel Cobalt Aluminum Oxide (NCA) delivers high energy density and power, primarily used in high-performance applications.</p></p>
